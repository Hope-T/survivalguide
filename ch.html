Char'Lesia Hughes


My name is Char’lesia Hughes I am 16 years old and I am from Chicago. I attend The Noble Academy, a charter school, so we have a lot of rules and regulations that must be followed and a strong disciplinary culture. I come from a big family. I have five siblings on my mom side and six on my dad side of the family.I have done cheerleading for 2 years and counting and step for 8 years. My zodiac sign is a libra. I love sports/clubs that involves or builds a sisterhood because I love it when other women power one another and supports them. I remember my sophomore year I tried to balance school while also doing cheerleading and maintaining a job. There was several times when I would go home crying because the pressure was too much. Then one day I came to school and my eyes were puffy and red and my advisor asked me, “Char’lesia what’s wrong?” I didn’t know what to say, so I broke down and said, “I can’t do it, but I can’t let them down,” at this point she began to cry and told me to tell her everything. I finally told her everything and it felt like a big burden had been lifted off of me. I then realized that I thought I knew how to balance school, personal life, and a job, but I really didn’t and that was okay because I was just a sophomore at the time.

1.Never be afraid to ask for help, whether it's from a friend, counselor, teacher, parents, or siblings.

2.Avoid peer pressure by sticking to who you are as a person and your values, so say no when ever you don’t agree with something. .

3.NEVER overload yourself, so only join what you have time for and save one day out the week for some free time.

4.Always have your end goal in mind, it will help to avoid drama and evaluating your friends will also help to maintain your goals.

5.Don’t be afraid to ask for advice, you’ll be surprised by how someone else’s advice about a situation can affect the outcomes and how they help make your high school life more easier.
