Shaping Yourself


Sophomore year makes you feel out of place, however, it is also the year that you discover who you are. In order to shape yourself into a successful person, you must surround yourself those who aspire to become a better person in life. You must surround yourself with people encourages you and uplifts you. When choosing your friends, ask your self, "Are my friends having a positive or negative influence on me?"; if not then you might want to reevaluate your friend group. It is also best to find a mentor, someone who is older than you. They can give you advice as to how to shape yourself into the best person you can be. Surrounding yourself around older people that has the same interests helps you get a upper hand.

Why is it Important to Shape Yourself?

Sophomore year is important for shaping yourself because you are no longer a new face, so people have a perception of who you are whether that’s good or bad you can change that.

Sophomore year can weird when it comes down to molding and shaping yourself because you’re not sure if you should start preparing yourself for college or if you should just have fun. The best way to mold and shape yourself is to have freedom while also preparing yourself for colleges by getting great grades and by taking or looking at standardized tests
