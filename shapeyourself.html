Shaping Yourself


Sophomore year can make you feel out of place; after all, you're not the newest, youngest kid on the block, nor are you the upperclassmen preparing for college. This is the year that you really discover who you are; this is also the year where you can try to change yourself into who you want to be without the stress of a completely new environment. In order to shape yourself into a successful person, you must surround yourself with those who aspire also to become better people in life. You must surround yourself with people who encourage you and uplift you. When choosing your friends, ask yourself, "Are my friends having a positive or negative influence on me?" If they are not being a positive influence on you, then you might want to reevaluate your friend group. It is also best to find a mentor, that is, someone who is older than you and can give you advice on how to shape yourself into the best person you can be. Surrounding yourself around older people who have the same interests can help you get an upper hand as well, as they can give you tried and successful advice.

Why is it Important to Shape Yourself?

Sophomore year is important for shaping yourself because you are no longer a new face-- people have a perception of who you are, and that can either be good or bad; this is the year to change before the stress of testing comes next year.
